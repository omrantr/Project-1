Places to Visit

The most exciting destinations, experiences, hidden gems, and traveler faves to check out now.

Nicosia: Kykkos Monastery

Located in the mountainous region of Marathasa Valley the Monastery of Kykkos is the wealthiest and most lavish on the island and stands on a mountain peak at an altitude of 1.318 metres.Dedicated to Panagia (the Virgin Mary), the monastery possesses one of three icons attributed to Agios Loukas (Apostle Luke) the Evangelist. The icon - covered in silver gilt - is in a shrine made of tortoiseshell and mother-of-pearl that stands in front of the iconostasis

Paphos: The Tombs of the Kings

The Tombs of the Kings in Paphos is an archaeological site dating back to ancient times in the Republic of Cyprus. Also known as the it is a collection of burial sites dating from the 4th to the 3rd century BCE.These tombs consist of a group of elaborate burial chambers that were used to bury nobles and officials during that period. Discovered in 1977 in the western part of modern-day Paphos, the Tombs of the Kings are among the significant archaeological sites in Cyprus.

Kourion: Roman Theater

It features a magnificent Roman theater dating back to the 2nd century AD.The theater is used for hosting artistic and cultural performances, as well as tourist events. Contains the remains of the ancient city, including houses, temples, baths, and other structures.Ongoing excavations reveal the evolution and prosperity of the city through the ages.

Ayia Napa

Ayia Napa is a fantastic tourist destination in Cyprus, renowned for its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife. Here are some remarkable areas and attractions in Ayia Napa:Ayia Napa is an excellent destination for tourists seeking the beauty of the beaches and an exciting nightlife experience in Cyprus.

Limassol: Old Port

The port also features a marina for luxury yachts, making it a beloved destination for yacht owners and sailing enthusiasts. In the past, the port was used for loading and unloading goods and maritime trade, reflecting Limassol's significance as a commercial center.

Larnaca: Church of Saint Lazarus

The Church of Saint Lazarus was built in the 9th century on a site believed to contain the tomb of Saint Lazarus, who is traditionally considered the person resurrected by Jesus Christ from the dead.The interior of the church is adorned with numerous icons and religious paintings dating back to the Byzantine period and traditional Eastern icons.

Held in May the festival includes colorful parades and floral decorations adorning the streets of Larnaca.

A distinctive event in Yierokipia featuring artistic performances and flower beauty contests.

Taking place in August in the gardens of Limassol visitors can taste a wide variety of Cypriot wines.
